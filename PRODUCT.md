# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective insurance agents (licensed or working toward licensing) who land on this page while evaluating teams to join. They're comparing agencies on credibility, culture, comp structure, mentorship, and tooling, and deciding whether it's worth booking an intro call with Jace Frenkel, the team leader. They're sizing up the agency the way they'd size up any career bet — quickly, skeptically, looking for signals of a serious operation vs. a recruiting funnel.

## Product Purpose

A recruiting landing page for Frenkel Financial Agency. It introduces Jace Frenkel, explains what the agency offers (mentorship, transparent commission levels, in-house CRM, licensing support, path to leadership), and converts visitors into a scheduled interview via the embedded Calendly widget (with a contact form as fallback). Success = a visitor leaves convinced this is a precise, well-run, selective operation worth their career bet, and books a call.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, confident, exclusive. The site should feel like a precision-run private firm that's selective about who it brings on — not a high-pressure recruiting funnel, not a soft "we're all family here" pitch. The new silver/chrome "F" mark sets a material language (graphite, brushed steel, specular highlight) that should carry through the whole site as a quality signal, used deliberately rather than slapped on as decoration. Confidence without flash.

## Anti-references

- Generic insurance/corporate sites: stock handshake photography, blue gradient heroes, "trust badge" rows, generic icon+text value props.
- SaaS startup templates: hero-metric stat blocks, gradient-clipped text, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow labels on every section, identical icon-grid feature cards.
- Fintech/crypto dark dashboards: neon glows, heavy glassmorphism, gradient-bordered cards.
- The site's *current* design is itself an anti-reference: navy/gold/cream with Fraunces + Sora is a competent but generic "premium template" formula (Fraunces is a training-data-default display serif). The reface should diffuse away from this, not refine it.

## Design Principles

- **Selectivity as design language** — the site should feel like it's evaluating the visitor as much as the reverse: confident, unhurried, never salesy.
- **Chrome is the motif, not a gimmick** — the metallic mark sets a graphite/brushed-steel/specular material language used sparingly and deliberately, not as a gradient applied everywhere.
- **One dominant idea per section** — long, deliberate scroll; art direction can vary section to section as long as the voice stays consistent.
- **Engineered motion** — sharp eases (no bounce/elastic), motion communicates precision and confidence, not playfulness.
- **Conversion stays frictionless** — the "Schedule an Interview" path stays clear and prominent even as the visual system gets bolder.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimums (body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1), including on dark/chrome surfaces where light text on dark needs extra line-height. Preserve and extend the existing `prefers-reduced-motion` handling — every new animation needs a reduced-motion fallback (crossfade or instant).
